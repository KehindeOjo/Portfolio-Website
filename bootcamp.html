No prior experience with Excel, Power BI, or data needed. We start from the fundamentals and build up.

Working Professionals

What the Training Covers

Learning should be practical. Training focuses on applying tools to real business scenarios, not just learning software in isolation.

Microsoft Excel for Data Analysis

Formulas, pivot tables, lookups, data cleaning, and building functional spreadsheets using real business datasets.

Power BI Dashboard Development

Data modeling, DAX basics, and building interactive dashboards you can present to employers confidently.

Data Cleaning & Preparation

Identifying, handling, and transforming messy data into structured, analysis-ready datasets.

Business Reporting

Designing clear, professional reports that communicate findings to stakeholders and decision-makers.

Data Visualization

Choosing the right chart types and visual elements to make data insights immediately clear and actionable.

Analytics Projects

End-to-end project work using real datasets so you leave with portfolio pieces you can show employers.

Career Guidance for Aspiring Analysts

CV building, LinkedIn positioning, portfolio setup, and interview preparation for data roles.

Why Learn With Me

I Teach What I Use on the Job, Not Just Theory

I am a working Data Analyst, not a full-time trainer running a side hustle. Every dataset and dashboard exercise in KO DataLab comes from the same kind of problems I solve for organizations day to day, financial reporting, audits, and process automation. That is the gap most beginner courses miss: they teach tools in isolation, without showing how analysts actually use them to answer real business questions.
